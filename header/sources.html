robotics
is the field of engineering and science dedicated to the design, construction, operation, and application of robots.
This discipline overlaps with electronics, computer science, artificial intelligence, mechatronics, nanotechnology, and bioengineering. Here are some key aspects of robotechnics:
  1. Design and Construction: This involves creating the physical form and structure of robots. Engineers use principles from mechanical engineering to design robots' moving parts and frameworks.
  2. Control Systems: This involves programming the robot to perform tasks. Control systems can range from simple manual controls to complex algorithms processed by a computer.
  3. Sensors and Actuators: Robots use sensors to interact with their environment and actuators to move or perform actions. Sensors can include cameras, microphones, or various environmental sensors, while actuators might be motors or hydraulic systems.
  4. Artificial Intelligence (AI): In advanced robotics, AI is used to process information from sensors and make decisions or learn from experiences. This can include machine learning, computer vision, and natural language processing.
